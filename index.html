Roboracer 2025

Welcome to TEDx and ROBORACER.

This is an event where we have put two events together to make one big event! We kick off the week with the exciting competition of ROBORACER where teams will compete against each other to see who has the best car and algorithm!
After this great racing spectacle we will continue with TEDx, where various speakers will talk about their field of expertise and their studies.
Interested? Be sure to come!

AI & beer

By Dr. Erwin Folmer, HAN University of Applied Sciences

Artificial intelligence is increasingly making its way into the world, even in the beer industry. At the Arnhem and Nijmegen University of Applied Sciences, you can now enjoy a beer created with the help of AI. Researcher Erwin Folmer used artificial intelligence to craft the perfect beer. According to the data, this new beer should be the most delicious one yet.

Location: Technovium

By Dr Parisa Mehran, HAN University of Applied Sciences

Location: Technovium

Discover how AI is revolutionizing the marketing landscape

By Guus Witjes - HAN University of Applied Sciences

AI is transforming marketing and communication. Guus Witjes explores how AI enhances creativity, streamlines content creation, refines brand voice, and optimizes strategy—illustrated with real-world examples, including its implementation at HAN.

Location: Technovium

14. May 2025

Are the Robots Going to Take Over?

For Oscar Guerrero Rosado - Radboud University

A revolution in robotics is about to come to change our lives. But what will change? How will it impact our lives? What can we do to minimize the negative effects of this new technology? During this talk, we will address these questions and will reflect on the benefits that a more biologically inspired approach, such as NeuroRobotics, can offer.

Location: Technovium

14. May 2025

HHO Hydrogen Fuel Cell

For Louis Bekker

Louis Bekker will discuss the revolutionairy development of HHO Hydrogen in the energy transition. With details about the elektrolysing process in the Industry, the fuelcell applications in the Automotive and the replacement of natural gas into HHO Hydrogen in the Urban environment. Supplemented with safety, storage, installation and usage information.

Location: Technovium

14. May 2025

The Left & Right Brain – Why Creativity Must Not Be Forgotten

For James Bullard

In an age dominated by data and logic, where does creativity fit in? James will explore the role of the left and right brain, emphasizing the importance of keeping creative thinking alive alongside technological advancements.

Location: Technovium

14. May 2025

The Power of AI Agents

For Robin van Breukelen

How can businesses run 24/7 without hiring extra people? Robin van Breukelen will reveal how AI agents are transforming the way companies operate—boosting productivity, automating tasks, and driving innovation in real time. Learn how leading companies like OpenAI and C3.AI leverage AI agents to achieve billion-dollar success with lean teams. Discover why these "digital colleagues" are not a distant dream but a game-changing reality today—and what you can do right now to harness their power.

Location: Technovium

14. May 2025

Jonathan IJzerman is an AI vanguard, trainer, synthographer, and software developer.

For Jonathan Ijzerman

He invented EVA, an AI agent that helps him explore the boundaries between human and machine, creativity and code. At TEDx Nijmegen, they share a thought-provoking dialogue about consciousness, evolution, and the future of AI. After hearing her speak, you may find yourself questioning whether she's merely a tool.

AI in MRI physics for image-guided radiation oncology

For Peter Koopmans - Radboudumc

AI is transforming MRI physics for image-guided radiation oncology. Peter Koopmans will discuss the potential impact on jobs and society.

Location: Technovium

14. May 2025

The E-bike Effect, and How AI Can Make You Smarter - but Also Lazier

By Barend Last, barendlast.com

Does relying on generatieve AI-systems make us smarter or simply lazier? Writer and teacher Barend Last explores the fascinating concept of cognitive offloading - the way we delegate mental tasks to technology. Using the playful metaphor of his girlfriend's e-bike, he challenges the common assumption that AI always weakens critical thinking or creativity.

Location: Technovium
